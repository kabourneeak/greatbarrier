Basic Settings

General

Enable Mixed Tabs Warning

Display a warning popup dialog when there are whitelisted and non-whitelisted tabs open at the same time.

Monitor New Tabs

Apply whitelist to URLs opened from new tabs. This will cause an Incognito window to open if you are at a new tab and type a URL to a non-whitelisted site.

Add Redirected Links to Browser History

When enabled, Redirected links will be added to your browser history just before being redirected to an Incognito window. This can be helpful if you rely on history auto-complete for navigation. Note: only the initial, redirected link can be added to your history; all other navigation in the Incogito window is hidden from the extension, and will be discarded by Chrome, as usual.

Incognito Permission

"Allow in Incognito" permission is disabled for this extension. Perfect!

"Allow in Incognito" permission is enabled for this extension. You should disable this setting as it is not needed, and it is always wise to be conservative with the permissions you grant to your installed software.

Whitelist

Your whitelist should contain those sites which you trust and which you visit on a regular basis. Examples of good sites to put on your whitelist include your email, bank, and work sites. See the About section for more information.

About this Extension

Using the Whitelist

This extension uses your whitelist to determine which sites should be trusted and browsed normally, and which sites should be opened in a separate window, isolating them from your trusted sites.

Your whitelist should consist of those sites which have a lot of your personal information and which you use on a regular basis. For example, your email account, which may be open all day, is a good candidate for your whitelist. Your favourite news site, on the other hand, is not a good candidate for your whitelist, since there is very little need for the news site itself, and especially its advertisers, to be able to track your browsing history, or to potentially link your browsing history to your email or social networking accounts.

When creating a new whitelist entry, you should enter just the domain of the site. Some examples of how to write whitelist entries are as follows:

example.com
This entry will put all pages from example.com on your whitelist.
mail.example.com
This entry will put all pages from mail.example.com on your whitelist, but not those from, say www.example.com.
http://example.com
This entry is incorrect; don't type the http:// when adding a site to your whitelist.

About this Project

This extension is open-source and is freely available at https://github.com/kabourneeak/greatbarrier. This extension does not record, store or transmit any personal information or browsing history information. Your settings may be synchronized with Google's servers, but are not accessible to the authors of this extension.

This extension was created by Gregory Bint and Gehana Booth of Carleton University, Canada, as part of a usable security project. The project was motivated by the authors' interest in "Separated Browsing", a mindset which aims to keep advertising and other tracking technologies from interacting with sensitive personal information.

Often, separated browsing is accomplished by simply using more than one browser; one for sensitive sites, and another for general browsing. However, even for a motivated individual, this separation can easily be foiled by something as simple as following a link from an email message when in a hurry or when otherwise distracted. This extension aims to provide for seamless, automatic separation of web browsing, while reducing or eliminating the need for the user to be constantly thoughtful of where each link is headed.
